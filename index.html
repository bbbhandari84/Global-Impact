Who We Are

A Global Platform That Turns Development Gaps Into Enduring Solutions

Global Impact & Adoption Alliance (GIAA) is a Canada-based nonprofit operating as a multi-domain impact platform. Not a single-project organisation. GIAA identifies structural gaps across global development systems and deploys targeted solutions through three areas: global policy frameworks, community field models, and knowledge & capacity building.

Projects are applications of this platform. Each is co-designed with local stakeholders, grounded in evidence, and built to outlast the project cycle. GIAA's active initiatives include the Post-Project Continuity & Adoption Framework (PCAF) and Bhutanese for Bhutanese: Local Inclusive Value Chain Initiative, with further projects in development across all three verticals.

GIAA Project 01 · Governance & Transition

Project Continuity and Adoption Framework (PCAF)

The framework that embeds transition governance into development projects — so outcomes survive funding exit.

The PCAF is GIAA's flagship governance project. It is a four-component framework that embeds transition planning, coordination, and continuity financing into every stage of a project's lifecycle, so handover to ministry-led systems is structured, not improvised.

Project Continuity & Adoption Framework (PCAF)

A lifecycle-based framework integrating sustainability from design through to post-project phases.

2

National Impact Adoption Coordination (NIAC)

A ministry-embedded coordination function responsible for sector-level continuity. Not a new institution.

3

Sector-Based Pooled Continuity Mechanism (SPCM)

A pooled approach to sustainability financing across multiple projects within a sector.

4

Post-Project Sustainability Allocation (PPSA)

A dedicated allocation (5–10% of project budget) integrated at design stage to fund post-project continuity.

Founder & Executive Director

Bal Bhandari

Global Impact and Adoption Alliance

Bal Bhandari is the Founder and Lead of the Global Impact and Adoption Alliance (GIAA), an initiative dedicated to strengthening post-project sustainability through governance-driven implementation frameworks. His work focuses on addressing a critical gap in development programming: ensuring that impact continues beyond project completion through structured institutional ownership and long-term system integration.

With over 10 years of international experience across Bhutan, Europe, and Canada, Bal brings a multidisciplinary background spanning veterinary science, sustainable livestock systems, animal nutrition, and climate-aligned agriculture. His expertise integrates field implementation, institutional program leadership, and applied research across diverse development contexts.

He previously served as Program Director at the Regional Livestock Development Center (RLDC), Bhutan, where he led multiple national and international development projects. In this role, he coordinated multi-stakeholder initiatives involving government agencies, development partners, and local communities, with a focus on livestock development, animal health systems, and rural livelihood enhancement.

Bal's current work challenges conventional sustainability models that prioritise short-term outputs over long-term system resilience. Through GIAA, he is advancing a governance-led approach that embeds sustainability as a structured function within national systems.

He holds advanced training in sustainable animal nutrition from leading European institutions and has engaged in international research collaborations across France, Denmark, and Hungary, contributing to advancements in feed innovation, methane mitigation strategies, and dairy productivity optimisation.
