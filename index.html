About GIAA

Bridging the Gap Between Project Exit and Lasting Impact

GIAA is a Canada-based nonprofit addressing the most overlooked gap in global development: the lack of continuity after funding ends. Most development projects achieve strong results during implementation — then disappear. Communities are left without services. Investments are lost. GIAA exists to change that.

The Challenge

The Post-Project Sustainability Gap

Across agriculture, education, health, and environment — most projects are funded for 2–5 years. When funding ends, the communities they served are often left behind.

  • ✗ No structured exit or handover plans
  • ✗ Local capacity rarely built during implementation
  • ✗ Infrastructure abandoned, services discontinued
  • ✗ Development investment fails to deliver its full return

The GIAA Response

Lifecycle Continuity, Not Just Delivery

GIAA operates as a full lifecycle partner — present from MoU design through transition and long-term adoption, through NIACs embedded in each country.

  • ✓ Structured adoption at project close
  • ✓ Dedicated sector specialists per country
  • ✓ Self-sustaining sector continuity fund
  • ✓ Impact verified at 1, 3, and 5 years

Project Takeover & Transition Mechanism

GIAA follows a structured five-step process for identifying, evaluating, and formally adopting projects nearing or past completion.

1
Identification & Screening

Projects identified prior to or immediately after closure. Initial screening for impact potential and operational feasibility.

2
Detailed Evaluation

Technical, financial, and operational assessment. Compliance review with donor and regulatory requirements. Stakeholder consultations.

3
Compliance Review

Verification of project completion status, fund utilization, and outputs delivered. Full documentation of performance and lessons learned.

4
Handover Agreement

Formal transition MoU between original stakeholders and GIAA/NIAC. Clear definition of roles, responsibilities, timelines, and the continuity plan.

5
✅ Adoption & Continuity Activated

Long-term support mechanisms activated. Sector specialist takes formal ownership. Monitoring, reporting, and scaling strategies begin — indefinitely.

Self-Sustaining by Design

GIAA's operational sustainability is built into every project from day one — not negotiated after completion. A dual-stream commitment ensures a Sector Continuity Fund is active in every country GIAA operates in, without dependence on new grant cycles.

Stream 1 Donor Commitment

A portion of each project grant is ring-fenced for long-term adoption — agreed at MOU stage, before disbursement begins.

Stream 2 Residual Transfer

Unspent funds at project closeout flow into the NIAC Sector Continuity Fund — capturing value that would otherwise be returned or absorbed.

Impact Continuity

Proof of Impact — Not a Report of Successful Exit

Success is not a project closure report. Every project GIAA adopts is monitored, validated, and reported at 1, 3, and 5 years post-transition — because accountability means proving outcomes endure, not just that funding was spent.

🔍
Continuous Field Monitoring

A dedicated sector specialist maintains ongoing field presence, tracking whether intended outcomes — health improvements, crop yields, school enrolment — are sustained and progressing over time.

📋
Verifiable Impact Records

GIAA maintains structured impact records for every adopted project — documented evidence that goes beyond narrative reports to include field data, community feedback, and measurable indicators.

🤝
Local Ownership & Capacity

GIAA works alongside local governments, institutions, and communities — not above them. The goal is to build the local capacity to sustain outcomes independently, with GIAA as a long-term partner, not a perpetual external manager.

💰
Sector Continuity Funding

Each adopted project activates a dedicated sector fund at the NIAC level — financing monitoring, field validation, and operational continuity without dependence on new grant cycles or donor renewals.

GIAA's commitment to impact does not expire at project handover. Through structured adoption, specialist ownership, and a self-sustaining sector fund, GIAA ensures that every development investment continues to deliver — for the communities that depend on it, and for the donors and governments that made it possible. Impact beyond Exit is a standard, not a statement.

Organizational Structure

GIAA operates across five tiers — from global governance down to individual project portfolios. Each sector specialist owns one domain exclusively, with zero cross-sector multitasking and a dedicated Sector Continuity Fund financing long-term monitoring.
