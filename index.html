Who We Are

Bridging the Gap Between Project Exit and Lasting Impact

Global Impact & Adoption Alliance (GIAA) is a Canada-based Nonprofit Organization developing the Post-Project Transition Governance (PPTG) framework — a globally replicable system that embeds structured continuity mechanisms into development projects, enabling ministries to act as long-term custodians of outcomes across five development sectors.

Vision

To create a global ecosystem where development projects continue to deliver impact beyond their initial lifecycle, becoming sustainable, scalable, and community-owned.

Mission

To co-design, pilot, and scale the Project Continuity and Adoption Framework (PCAF) — partnering with governments, donors, and development agencies to embed structured transition governance into national systems, so ministries become the long-term custodians of development outcomes.

Sector Continuity Fund Model

Instead of leaving sustainability to chance at project closure, GIAA integrates two predictable funding streams — a design-stage allocation and a closeout optimisation mechanism — coordinated at sector level through the NIAC Function, within existing government and donor financial systems.

●
Governance Principle

All sustainability resources remain within government systems or project funding agency-managed financial mechanisms at all times. GIAA does not hold, manage, or control project funds. GIAA's role is framework design, coordination support, and transition facilitation only.

Dual-Stream Sustainability Integration Approach

Stream 1 Sustainability Allocation at Project Design Stage

A defined portion of project resources (typically 5–10% or a context-specific allocation) is incorporated into project design to support transition, adoption, and post-project continuity.

  • ›Integrated within donor-approved budgets and funding agreements
  • ›Allocated for transition, monitoring, validation, and adoption activities
  • ›Aligned with project results frameworks and sustainability objectives
  • ›Implemented under existing donor and implementing agency financial systems

Sustainability is planned, resourced, and measurable from the outset, not dependent on post-project funding gaps.

Stream 2 Closeout Resource Optimisation

At project completion, opportunities may be identified, subject to donor policies and financial regulations, to enhance continuity and extend impact. This may include:

  • ›Programmatic alignment of unutilised resources where permissible
  • ›Extension of key interventions supporting long-term outcomes
  • ›Reinforcement of sector-level continuity functions

Any such allocation:

  • ✓Is fully compliant with donor agreements
  • ✓Requires mutual agreement between funding and implementing partners
  • ✓Is transparently governed and auditable

Governance Framework

The model operates within established institutional and financial structures:

Ministry Ownership

Ministries serve as sector owners and lead the adoption process, maintaining full institutional authority over post-project systems and assets.

Donor & Agency Control

Donors and implementing agencies retain full financial control and oversight. No funds are transferred to or managed by GIAA.

GIAA's Role

Framework design, coordination support, and transition facilitation only. GIAA does not manage, disburse, or hold project or sector funds.

Oversight Mechanisms

Multi-stakeholder governance structures, transparent reporting, and independent auditing in accordance with all partner requirements.

Implementation Logic

Project Design
Sustainability allocation integrated into budget and results framework (Stream 1)
Implementation
Systems strengthened, documented, and transition readiness monitored
Project Completion
Closeout resource optimisation where applicable and compliant (Stream 2)
NIAC Coordination
Sector-level continuity monitoring, validation, and ongoing support activated
Outcome
Sustained impact beyond the project lifecycle, with institutional ownership and efficient resource use

Impact Continuity

Proof of Impact — Not a Report of Successful Exit

GIAA measures success by evidence that endures — not by the moment a project closes. Every country where the PCAF framework is implemented enters a structured continuity cycle: outcomes monitored by the ministry-embedded NIAC coordinator, validated through field evidence, and reported at regular intervals. Impact beyond Exit is a standard, not a statement.

🔍
Field Monitoring

The ministry-embedded NIAC coordinator tracks sustained outcomes — crop yields, school enrolment, health indicators — through ongoing field presence, supported by GIAA's evaluation framework.

📋
Verifiable Impact Records

Structured impact records for every PCAF-implemented project: field data, community feedback, and measurable indicators — maintained by the NIAC Function within the ministry, not external reports.

🤝
Local Ownership

GIAA equips ministries and communities with the governance structures to sustain outcomes independently. The NIAC Function becomes an internal ministry capability — GIAA provides the framework and technical guidance, not operational management.

💰
Continuity Funding

The PPSA allocation (5–10% of project budget) and the SPCM pooling mechanism are integrated into government and donor financial systems during framework design — financing post-project monitoring and continuity without dependency on new grant cycles.

Global Reach

Global Relevance & Scalability

The PCAF model is built for replication. A standardised governance approach means any government, donor, or NGO can deploy it regardless of region — adapting the NIAC Function to local ministry structures while maintaining a consistent coordination standard globally.

The model scales progressively: from a single sector pilot to cross-sector integration, then to national development programming. Each stage builds on validated outcomes, compounding impact as more countries adopt the framework and the SPCM evidence base expands.

Standardised Framework

A single, consistent approach to transition governance means development partners, governments, and NGOs can apply the same PCAF model regardless of region, reducing setup time and increasing predictability of outcomes.

Locally Embedded, Globally Consistent

The NIAC Function adapts to each country's own ministry structures, governance language, and sector priorities, while maintaining a consistent coordination standard across all GIAA operations worldwide.

Sector Continuity Scales with Adoption

The SPCM pooling model strengthens as more countries implement the framework. Each new PCAF activation adds to the evidence base, refines the governance approach, and demonstrates replicability — compounding global impact over time.
